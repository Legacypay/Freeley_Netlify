## Skipping the Waiting Room

For decades, getting treatment for stigmatized male health issues like hair loss and erectile dysfunction meant uncomfortable clinic visits, waiting in lobbies, and high pharmacy copays.

Today, telehealth is changing that. But not all telehealth platforms are created equal.

### 1. Physician-Led Protocols
Many popup websites offer generic medications with little to no clinical oversight. A reputable telehealth platform should require a thorough intake and have actual, board-certified physicians review your medical history.

### 2. The Power of Compounding
Why settle for standard generic pills when compounding pharmacies can customize your treatment? For example, **Freeley** utilizes a 3-in-1 sublingual troche for ED that dissolves under the tongue, bypassing the digestive system for a 15-minute onset rather than the standard hour. 

### Conclusion

Your time matters, and your health isn't something to take a risk on with gray-market websites. Look for clinical platforms that offer transparency, compounding capabilities, and direct medical oversight.
