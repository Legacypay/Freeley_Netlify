Medical Education

How does it work?

Most male pattern baldness is caused by genetics and a hormone called DHT. Our treatments target the root cause while stimulating blood flow to bring dormant follicles back to life.

Block DHT

Finasteride blocks the 5-alpha-reductase enzyme, reducing DHT levels in the scalp by up to 60%. This stops the miniaturization of hair follicles and halts further shedding.

Boost Blood Flow

Minoxidil acts as a vasodilator. It expands the blood vessels in the scalp, delivering vital oxygen, blood, and nutrients directly to the follicles, pushing them back into the active growth phase.

Thicken & Strengthen

Over 3 to 6 months of consistent use, miniaturized hairs are replaced by thicker, darker, and stronger terminal hairs, fundamentally increasing the density and coverage of your hair.

Common Questions

Everything you
need to know

Hair growth takes time. You may notice less shedding within the first few months, but visible regrowth and thickening typically takes 3 to 6 months of consistent daily use. Maximum results are usually seen after 1 year.
If you stop treatment, the DHT will return to your scalp and the hair follicles will begin shrinking again. You will likely lose any hair you've regrown within 6 to 12 months, returning to your baseline.
Finasteride is generally well tolerated. A small percentage of men (around 1-2%) may experience sexual side effects such as decreased libido, which typically resolve upon stopping the medication. Minoxidil may cause mild scalp irritation.
It is common to experience increased shedding during the first 2 to 6 weeks. This means the medication is working! Your follicles are pushing out old, thin hairs to make way for new, thicker growth. Do not panic—stay consistent.
